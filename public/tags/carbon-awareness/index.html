Carbon aware spatial shifting of Kubernetes workloads using Karmada

In my last post I looked at temporal shifting of kubernetes workloads using KEDA and the carbon-aware-keda-operator from Microsoft. With this approach non time sensitive workloads can be run at times when the carbon intensity of the electricity grid being used is lowest. In this post we’ll look at spatial shifting, this involves shifting workloads to physical locations where the grid carbon intensity is lower. This allows workloads that are more time sensitive to be made carbon aware....
